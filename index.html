About

I'm a final-year Computer Engineering student at TED University with a focus on ICS/OT and industrial network security. My background spans electrical-electronic systems, control technologies, and field-oriented security analysis — giving me a grounded perspective on how critical infrastructure can be both built and broken.

I've interned at Cyberwise/ICSFusion conducting traffic analysis on Modbus TCP/IP-based systems, at CenterSAU supporting cybersecurity R&D, and at Etiya working within agile software development processes. Earlier, I worked as a Control Automation Technician in military-standard production environments. Currently serving as Education Coordinator at CyberTEDU, I'm interested in the intersection of AI and offensive/defensive security — particularly how machine learning reshapes the threat landscape in industrial systems.
